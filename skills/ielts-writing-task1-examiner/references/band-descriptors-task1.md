# IELTS Writing Task 1 Band Descriptors

*Source: IELTS Writing Band Descriptors, Updated May 2023*
*Applies to both Academic (AC) and General Training (GT) Task 1*

> A script must fully fit the **positive** features of the descriptor at a particular level. **Bold text** indicates negative features that will limit a rating.

## Band Descriptors Table

| Band | Task Achievement | Coherence & Cohesion | Lexical Resource | Grammatical Range & Accuracy |
|------|-----------------|----------------------|-----------------|------------------------------|
| **9** | All requirements fully and appropriately satisfied. Extremely rare lapses in content. | Message can be followed effortlessly. Cohesion rarely attracts attention. Lapses minimal. Paragraphing skilfully managed. | Full flexibility and precise use. Wide range of vocabulary used accurately and appropriately with very natural and sophisticated control. Spelling/word formation errors extremely rare, minimal impact. | Wide range of structures used with full flexibility and control. Punctuation and grammar used appropriately throughout. Errors extremely rare, minimal impact. |
| **8** | Covers all requirements appropriately, relevantly and sufficiently. (AC) Key features skilfully selected, clearly presented, highlighted and illustrated. (GT) All bullet points clearly presented and appropriately illustrated or extended. Occasional omissions or lapses. | Message can be followed with ease. Ideas logically sequenced, cohesion well managed. Occasional lapses may occur. Paragraphing used sufficiently and appropriately. | Wide resource fluently and flexibly used to convey precise meanings. Skilful use of uncommon/idiomatic items when appropriate, despite occasional inaccuracies. Occasional spelling/word formation errors, minimal impact. | Wide range of structures flexibly and accurately used. Majority of sentences error-free, punctuation well managed. Occasional non-systematic errors, minimal impact. |
| **7** | Covers requirements of the task. Content relevant and accurate — few omissions or lapses. Format appropriate. (AC) Key features covered and clearly highlighted but could be more fully illustrated. Clear overview; data appropriately categorised; main trends/differences identified. (GT) All bullet points covered and clearly highlighted. Clear purpose. Tone consistent and appropriate. | Ideas logically organised with clear progression throughout. A few lapses may occur. Range of cohesive devices including reference and substitution used flexibly but with some inaccuracies or over/under use. | Sufficient resource for some flexibility and precision. Some ability to use less common/idiomatic items. Awareness of style and collocation evident, though inappropriacies occur. Only a few spelling/word formation errors, do not detract from clarity. | Variety of complex structures used with some flexibility and accuracy. Grammar and punctuation generally well controlled; error-free sentences frequent. A few errors may persist but do not impede communication. |
| **6** | Focuses on requirements; appropriate format used. (AC) Key features covered and adequately highlighted. Relevant overview attempted. Information appropriately selected and supported with figures/data. (GT) All bullet points covered and adequately highlighted. Purpose generally clear; may be minor tone inconsistencies. Some irrelevant/inaccurate information may occur in detail. Some details may be missing or excessive. | Information generally arranged coherently with clear overall progression. Cohesive devices used to some good effect but cohesion within/between sentences may be faulty or mechanical (misuse, overuse or omission). Reference and substitution may lack flexibility or clarity, resulting in some repetition or error. | Generally adequate and appropriate. Meaning generally clear despite restricted range or lack of precision. Risk-takers may use wider vocabulary but with higher inaccuracy. Some spelling/word formation errors but do not impede communication. | Mix of simple and complex sentence forms but flexibility limited. Complex structures not as accurate as simple ones. Errors in grammar and punctuation occur but rarely impede communication. |
| **5** | Generally addresses requirements; format may be inappropriate. (AC) Key features not adequately covered; recounting of detail mainly mechanical; **may be no data to support the description**. (GT) All bullet points presented but one or more may not be adequately covered; purpose may be unclear; tone may be variable/inappropriate. Tendency to focus on details without referring to bigger picture. Irrelevant/inaccurate material detracts from achievement. Limited detail when illustrating main points. | Organisation evident but not wholly logical; may lack overall progression. Nevertheless sense of underlying coherence. Relationship of ideas can be followed but sentences not fluently linked. May be limited/overuse of cohesive devices with some inaccuracy. Writing may be repetitive due to inadequate/inaccurate use of reference and substitution. | Limited but minimally adequate. Simple vocabulary may be used accurately but range does not permit much variation. Frequent lapses in appropriacy of word choice; lack of flexibility apparent in frequent simplifications/repetitions. Spelling/word formation errors may be noticeable and cause some difficulty for reader. | Range of structures limited and rather repetitive. Complex sentences attempted but tend to be faulty; greatest accuracy achieved on simple sentences. Grammatical errors may be frequent and cause some difficulty. Punctuation may be faulty. |
| **4** | Attempt to address the task. (AC) Few key features selected. (GT) Not all bullet points presented; purpose of letter not clearly explained and may be confused; tone may be inappropriate. **Format may be inappropriate.** Key features/bullet points may be irrelevant, repetitive, inaccurate or inappropriate. | Ideas evident but not arranged coherently; no clear progression. Relationships between ideas unclear and/or inadequately marked. Some use of basic cohesive devices, which may be inaccurate or repetitive. Inaccurate use or lack of substitution or referencing. | **Limited and inadequate for or unrelated to the task.** Vocabulary basic and may be used repetitively. May be inappropriate use of lexical chunks (memorised phrases, formulaic language). Inappropriate word choice and/or errors in word formation/spelling may impede meaning. | Very limited range of structures. **Subordinate clauses rare; simple sentences predominate.** Some structures produced accurately but grammatical errors frequent and may impede meaning. Punctuation often faulty or inadequate. |
| **3** | Does not address requirements (possibly misunderstood data/diagram/situation). Key features may be largely irrelevant. Limited information, may be used repetitively. | No apparent logical organisation. Ideas discernible but difficult to relate to each other. Minimal use of sequencers or cohesive devices. Those used do not necessarily indicate logical relationship. Difficulty in identifying referencing. | Inadequate (may be due to being significantly underlength). Possible over-dependence on input material or memorised language. Word choice/spelling very limited; errors predominate and may severely impede meaning. | Sentence forms attempted but errors in grammar and punctuation predominate (except in memorised phrases or input material). Prevents most meaning from coming through. **Length may be insufficient** to provide evidence of control. |
| **2** | Content barely relates to the task. | Little relevant message, or **entire response may be off-topic**. Little evidence of control of organisational features. | Extremely limited with few recognisable strings apart from memorised phrases. No apparent control of word formation and/or spelling. | Little or no evidence of sentence forms (except in memorised phrases). |
| **1** | **Responses of 20 words or fewer.** Content wholly unrelated to task. Copied rubric must be discounted. | Writing fails to communicate any message; appears to be by a virtual non-writer. | No resource apparent, except a few isolated words. | No rateable language evident. |
| **0** | Did not attend or attempt the question; used a language other than English throughout; or answer has been totally memorised. |||

## Scoring Notes

- Each of the 4 criteria is weighted equally for Task 1
- Overall Task 1 band = average of TA + CC + LR + GRA (rounded to nearest 0.5)
- Minimum word count: **150 words** (under-length responses will be penalised across all criteria)
- Scores are given in whole or half bands (e.g. 6.5, 7.0, 7.5)
