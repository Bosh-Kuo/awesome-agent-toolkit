# IELTS Writing Key Assessment Criteria — Task 2

*Source: IELTS.org — applies to both Academic (AC) and General Training (GT)*

## Task 2 Criteria

Task 2 is assessed on four equal criteria:

1. **Task Response (TR)**
2. **Coherence and Cohesion (CC)**
3. **Lexical Resource (LR)**
4. **Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA)**

> Task 2 carries **more weight** in the overall IELTS Writing band score than Task 1.

---

### Task Response (TR) — Task 2

Assesses how fully, appropriately, and relevantly the candidate addresses the prompt (minimum 250 words).

TR assesses ability to:
- Address all parts of the prompt
- Present a clear and developed position in response to the question
- Extend and support main ideas with relevant evidence and examples
- Maintain focus — avoid over-generalisation or unsupported assertions
- Use an appropriate format for the essay type (argumentative, discussion, problem-solution, etc.)

**Key distinction from Task 1 (Task Achievement):** Task 1 requires accurate *reporting* of data; Task 2 requires *argumentation* — a position must be stated, developed, and supported throughout.

---

### Coherence and Cohesion (CC) — Task 2

Concerned with the overall organisation and logical development of the argument.

- **Coherence**: linking of ideas through logical sequencing and clear paragraphing
- **Cohesion**: varied and appropriate use of cohesive devices (logical connectors, conjunctions, pronouns)

CC assesses:
- Logical organisation of ideas with clear progression
- Effective paragraphing — each paragraph has a clear main idea
- Logical sequencing of ideas within and across paragraphs
- Flexible use of reference and substitution (e.g. definite articles, pronouns)
- Appropriate use of discourse markers to signal relationships and stages

> **Task 2-specific note:** Paragraphing is more explicitly penalised at lower bands in Task 2 than in Task 1. Band 4 may have "no paragraphing"; Band 5 "paragraphing may be inadequate or missing".

---

### Lexical Resource (LR) — Task 2

Refers to the range of vocabulary used and the accuracy and appropriacy of that use.

LR assesses:
- Range of general and topic-specific words used (e.g. use of synonyms to avoid repetition)
- Adequacy and appropriacy of vocabulary (topic-specific items, indicators of stance/attitude)
- Precision of word choice and expression
- Control and use of collocations, idiomatic expressions and sophisticated phrasing
- Density and communicative effect of errors in spelling and word formation

---

### Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA) — Task 2

Refers to the range and accurate use of grammatical resource at sentence level.

GRA assesses:
- Range and appropriacy of structures used (simple, compound and complex sentences)
- Accuracy of simple, compound and complex sentences
- Density and communicative effect of grammatical errors
- Accurate and appropriate use of punctuation

---

## General Notes

- Scripts may be penalised if: (a) partly or wholly plagiarised, or (b) not written as full, connected text (e.g. bullet points, note form are not appropriate)
- Task 2 uses **Task Response (TR)** instead of Task Achievement (TA); the other three criteria (CC, LR, GRA) share descriptors with Task 1 (with minor Task 2-specific additions in CC)
- Task 2 minimum word count is **250 words** (vs. 150 for Task 1)
- Task 2 carries more weight in the final Writing band score
