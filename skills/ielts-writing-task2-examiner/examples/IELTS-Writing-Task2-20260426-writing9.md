## Correction Analysis

### Task Response

**What was done well:**

- A clear position ("I partially agree") is stated in the introduction, which is appropriate for this question type.
- The acknowledgement that punishment has both positive and negative effects reflects some critical thinking.
- The body paragraphs are broadly on-topic.

**What needs improvement:**

- The question is in two parts. The **second question — "What sort of punishment should parents and teachers be allowed to use?" — is not meaningfully answered.** The only attempt is one example (confiscating toys), but the essay never makes a general recommendation or draws a boundary between acceptable and unacceptable punishment. At Band 6+, both parts must be addressed.
- The first body paragraph argues that punishment is a "double-edged sword" but does not clearly take a position — it simply lists pros and cons without developing the writer's own stance.
- The Harvard statistic ("approximately 50% of individuals...") is a vague, unverifiable claim. IELTS examiners are trained to flag over-generalised or unsupported statistics. It would be safer to write "research suggests" or use a general claim rather than inventing a specific percentage.
- "toddlers need to understand the rewards and consequences" — the question is specifically about punishment, not rewards. Introducing "rewards" without explaining the parallel system is a slight mismatch with the task.

### Coherence & Cohesion

**What was done well:**

- Four clear paragraphs are present: introduction, discussion of punishment's risks and benefits, suggested approach, and conclusion.
- The essay has an overall progression from the general need for discipline to the importance of using it carefully.
- Cohesive devices such as "Conversely," "Furthermore," "Indeed," and "However" are used to signal contrast, addition, and conclusion.

**What needs improvement:**

- **Vague paragraph 3 opening:** "To tackle such a negative behaviour" is unclear because the previous paragraph mentioned trauma and aggression caused _by punishment_, so the reader is not sure which behaviour is being tackled.
- **Local cohesion issue:** The sentence "small things can be a valuable lesson..." is difficult to follow because the phrase "having been taught by adults" attaches incorrectly to "small things."
- **Mechanical linking:** Some cohesive devices are accurate but slightly formulaic. The essay would flow better if transitions clarified the logical relationship between punishment, emotional harm, and gentle discipline.
- **Article error affecting cohesion:** "such a negative behaviour" should be "such negative behaviour" because `behaviour` is uncountable in this general sense.
