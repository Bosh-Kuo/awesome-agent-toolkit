## Correction Analysis

### Task Achievement

**What was done well:**

- The overview identifies the most important relationship between the two charts rather than simply listing figures.
- All six categories are mentioned in both charts, and the data is reported accurately.
- The second body paragraph makes several useful cross-chart comparisons, especially for other appliances, refrigeration, and heating.

**What needs improvement:**

- Lighting also doubles proportionally from 4% of energy use to 8% of emissions, which is a meaningful comparison that should be stated.
- The final sentence lists lighting and cooling together, but these categories behave differently: lighting doubles, while cooling changes only slightly from 2% to 3%.
- The response would be stronger if the second body paragraph grouped all categories with higher emissions than energy use before contrasting them with heating.

### Coherence & Cohesion

**What was done well:**

- The paragraph structure is clear: introduction, overview, energy use, and emissions.
- Linking phrases such as "Overall," "In contrast," "Regarding energy use," "Similarly," and "Conversely" help the reader follow the comparison.
- The order of categories in each body paragraph is easy to follow.

**What needs improvement:**

- The final sentence feels slightly detached from the comparison developed earlier. A stronger version would connect it back to the two charts, for example: "In both charts, cooling remains minor, whereas lighting's emissions share is twice its energy-use figure."
- Several one-word connectors are used in a row. More varied sentence patterns, such as "Whereas heating..." or "Although refrigeration...", would improve cohesion and grammatical range at the same time.
