# IELTS Writing Key Assessment Criteria

*Source: IELTS.org — applies to both Academic (AC) and General Training (GT)*

## Task 1 Criteria

Task 1 is assessed on four equal criteria:

1. **Task Achievement (TA)**
2. **Coherence and Cohesion (CC)**
3. **Lexical Resource (LR)**
4. **Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA)**

### Task Achievement (TA) — Task 1

Assesses how fully, appropriately, accurately, and relevantly the response fulfils the task requirements (minimum 150 words).

**Academic Writing Task 1** — information-transfer task based on a diagram, graph, table, chart, map, or other visual input. TA assesses ability to:
- Select key features of the information
- Provide sufficient detail to illustrate these features
- Report the information, figures and trends accurately
- Compare or contrast the information by highlighting identifiable trends, principal changes or differences (rather than mechanical description)
- Present the response in an appropriate format

**General Training Writing Task 1** — letter-writing task. TA assesses ability to:
- Clearly explain the purpose of the letter
- Fully address the three bullet-pointed requirements set out in the task
- Extend these three functions appropriately and relevantly
- Use an appropriate format for the letter
- Consistently use a tone appropriate to the task

---

### Coherence and Cohesion (CC) — Task 1 & 2

Concerned with the overall organisation and logical development of the message.

- **Coherence**: linking of ideas through logical sequencing
- **Cohesion**: varied and appropriate use of cohesive devices (logical connectors, conjunctions, pronouns)

CC assesses:
- Coherence via logical organisation of information/ideas or logical progression of argument
- Appropriate use of paragraphing for topic organisation and presentation
- Logical sequencing of ideas and/or information within and across paragraphs
- Flexible use of reference and substitution (e.g. definite articles, pronouns)
- Appropriate use of discourse markers to mark stages (e.g. *First of all*, *In conclusion*) and signal relationships (e.g. *as a result*, *similarly*)

---

### Lexical Resource (LR) — Task 1 & 2

Refers to the range of vocabulary used and the accuracy and appropriacy of that use.

LR assesses:
- Range of general words used (e.g. use of synonyms to avoid repetition)
- Adequacy and appropriacy of vocabulary (e.g. topic-specific items, indicators of writer's attitude)
- Precision of word choice and expression
- Control and use of collocations, idiomatic expressions and sophisticated phrasing
- Density and communicative effect of errors in spelling
- Density and communicative effect of errors in word formation

---

### Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA) — Task 1 & 2

Refers to the range and accurate use of grammatical resource at sentence level.

GRA assesses:
- Range and appropriacy of structures used (e.g. simple, compound and complex sentences)
- Accuracy of simple, compound and complex sentences
- Density and communicative effect of grammatical errors
- Accurate and appropriate use of punctuation

---

## General Notes

- Scripts may be penalised if: (a) partly or wholly plagiarised, or (b) not written as full, connected text (e.g. bullet points, note form are not appropriate)
- Task 2 carries more weight in marking than Task 1
- Task 2 uses **Task Response (TR)** instead of Task Achievement (TA); the other three criteria (CC, LR, GRA) are shared
