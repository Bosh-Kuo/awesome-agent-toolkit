# IELTS Writing Task 2 Band Descriptors

*Source: IELTS Writing Band Descriptors, Updated May 2023*
*Applies to both Academic (AC) and General Training (GT) Task 2*

> A script must fully fit the **positive** features of the descriptor at a particular level. **Bold text** indicates negative features that will limit a rating.
>
> Note: Task 2 uses **Task Response (TR)** instead of Task Achievement (TA). The other three criteria (CC, LR, GRA) share the same descriptors as Task 1 but with minor Task 2-specific paragraphing requirements in CC (Bands 4–6).

## Band Descriptors Table

| Band | Task Response | Coherence & Cohesion | Lexical Resource | Grammatical Range & Accuracy |
|------|--------------|----------------------|-----------------|------------------------------|
| **9** | Prompt appropriately addressed and explored in depth. Clear and fully developed position directly answers the question/s. Ideas relevant, fully extended and well supported. Lapses in content or support extremely rare. | Message can be followed effortlessly. Cohesion rarely attracts attention. Lapses minimal. Paragraphing skilfully managed. | Full flexibility and precise use widely evident. Wide range of vocabulary used accurately and appropriately with very natural and sophisticated control. Spelling/word formation errors extremely rare, minimal impact. | Wide range of structures used with full flexibility and control. Punctuation and grammar used appropriately throughout. Errors extremely rare, minimal impact. |
| **8** | Prompt appropriately and sufficiently addressed. Clear and well-developed position presented. Ideas relevant, well extended and supported. Occasional omissions or lapses in content. | Message can be followed with ease. Ideas logically sequenced, cohesion well managed. Occasional lapses may occur. Paragraphing used sufficiently and appropriately. | Wide resource fluently and flexibly used to convey precise meanings. Skilful use of uncommon/idiomatic items when appropriate, despite occasional inaccuracies. Occasional spelling/word formation errors, minimal impact. | Wide range of structures flexibly and accurately used. Majority of sentences error-free, punctuation well managed. Occasional non-systematic errors, minimal impact. |
| **7** | Main parts of the prompt appropriately addressed. Clear and developed position presented. Main ideas extended and supported but **may be a tendency to over-generalise or lack focus/precision** in supporting ideas/material. | Ideas logically organised with clear progression throughout. A few minor lapses may occur. Range of cohesive devices including reference and substitution used flexibly but with some inaccuracies or over/under use. Paragraphing generally used effectively; sequencing of ideas within a paragraph generally logical. | Sufficient resource for some flexibility and precision. Some ability to use less common/idiomatic items. Awareness of style and collocation evident, though inappropriacies occur. Only a few spelling/word formation errors, do not detract from clarity. | Variety of complex structures used with some flexibility and accuracy. Grammar and punctuation generally well controlled; error-free sentences frequent. A few errors may persist but do not impede communication. |
| **6** | Main parts of the prompt addressed (some may be more fully covered than others). Appropriate format used. Position directly relevant to prompt, though conclusions **may be unclear, unjustified or repetitive**. Main ideas relevant but **some may be insufficiently developed or lack clarity**; some supporting arguments/evidence may be less relevant or inadequate. | Information generally arranged coherently with clear overall progression. Cohesive devices used to some good effect but cohesion within/between sentences may be faulty or mechanical (misuse, overuse or omission). Reference and substitution may lack flexibility or clarity. **Paragraphing may not always be logical and/or the central topic may not always be clear.** | Generally adequate and appropriate. Meaning generally clear despite restricted range or lack of precision. Risk-takers may use wider vocabulary but with higher inaccuracy. Some spelling/word formation errors but do not impede communication. | Mix of simple and complex sentence forms but flexibility limited. Complex structures not as accurate as simple ones. Errors in grammar and punctuation occur but rarely impede communication. |
| **5** | Main parts of prompt **incompletely addressed**. Format may be inappropriate. Writer expresses a position but development not always clear. Some main ideas put forward but limited and not sufficiently developed; may be irrelevant detail. There may be some repetition. | Organisation evident but not wholly logical; may lack overall progression. Nevertheless sense of underlying coherence. Relationship of ideas can be followed but sentences not fluently linked. May be limited/overuse of cohesive devices with some inaccuracy. Writing may be repetitive due to inadequate/inaccurate use of reference and substitution. **Paragraphing may be inadequate or missing.** | Limited but minimally adequate. Simple vocabulary may be used accurately but range does not permit much variation. Frequent lapses in appropriacy of word choice; lack of flexibility apparent in frequent simplifications/repetitions. Spelling/word formation errors may be noticeable and cause some difficulty for reader. | Range of structures limited and rather repetitive. Complex sentences attempted but tend to be faulty; greatest accuracy achieved on simple sentences. Grammatical errors may be frequent and cause some difficulty. Punctuation may be faulty. |
| **4** | Prompt tackled in minimal way or answer is tangential, possibly due to misunderstanding. **Format may be inappropriate.** Position discernible, but reader has to read carefully to find it. Main ideas difficult to identify; identifiable ideas **may lack relevance, clarity and/or support**. Large parts may be repetitive. | Ideas evident but not arranged coherently; no clear progression. Relationships between ideas unclear and/or inadequately marked. Some use of basic cohesive devices, which may be inaccurate or repetitive. Inaccurate use or lack of substitution or referencing. **There may be no paragraphing and/or no clear main topic within paragraphs.** | **Limited and inadequate for or unrelated to the task.** Vocabulary basic and may be used repetitively. May be inappropriate use of lexical chunks (memorised phrases, formulaic language). Inappropriate word choice and/or errors in word formation/spelling **may impede meaning**. | Very limited range of structures. **Subordinate clauses rare; simple sentences predominate.** Some structures produced accurately but grammatical errors frequent and may impede meaning. Punctuation often faulty or inadequate. |
| **3** | No part of prompt adequately addressed, or prompt misunderstood. No relevant position identifiable; little direct response to question/s. Few ideas; may be irrelevant or insufficiently developed. | No apparent logical organisation. Ideas discernible but difficult to relate to each other. Minimal use of sequencers or cohesive devices. Those used do not necessarily indicate logical relationship. Difficulty in identifying referencing. Any attempts at paragraphing are unhelpful. | Inadequate (may be due to being significantly underlength). Possible over-dependence on input material or memorised language. Word choice/spelling very limited; errors predominate and **may severely impede meaning**. | Sentence forms attempted but errors in grammar and punctuation predominate (except in memorised phrases or input material). Prevents most meaning from coming through. **Length may be insufficient** to provide evidence of control. |
| **2** | Content barely related to prompt. No position can be identified. May be glimpses of one or two ideas without development. | Little relevant message, or **entire response may be off-topic**. Little evidence of control of organisational features. | Extremely limited with few recognisable strings apart from memorised phrases. No apparent control of word formation and/or spelling. | Little or no evidence of sentence forms (except in memorised phrases). |
| **1** | **Responses of 20 words or fewer.** Content wholly unrelated to prompt. Copied rubric must be discounted. | Writing fails to communicate any message; appears to be by a virtual non-writer. | No resource apparent, except a few isolated words. | No rateable language evident. |
| **0** | Did not attend or attempt the question; used a language other than English throughout; or answer has been totally memorised. |||

## Key Differences from Task 1

| Aspect | Task 1 | Task 2 |
|--------|--------|--------|
| First criterion name | Task Achievement (TA) | Task Response (TR) |
| Minimum word count | 150 words | **250 words** |
| TR/TA focus | Accurately report/compare visual data; include overview | Develop a clear position; extend and support main ideas |
| Over-generalisation risk | Not a concern | Flagged at Band 7 as a potential weakness |
| Paragraphing (Band 4–5) | Less explicitly penalised | Explicitly flagged: "no paragraphing" (Band 4), "inadequate or missing" (Band 5) |

## Scoring Notes

- Each of the 4 criteria is weighted equally for Task 2
- Overall Task 2 band = average of TR + CC + LR + GRA (rounded to nearest 0.5)
- Minimum word count: **250 words** (under-length responses will be penalised across all criteria)
- Scores are given in whole or half bands (e.g. 6.5, 7.0, 7.5)
- Task 2 carries **more weight** in the overall Writing band score than Task 1
